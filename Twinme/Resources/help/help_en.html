Getting Started

After entering your default “Profile” (name and image) used to “invite” your twinme contacts, you need to create your twinme contact list. Indeed, twinme does not use phone numbers or any other personal information that can identify you or your contacts. twinme does not peek into your address book, which contains personal information about your contacts that twinme could not use.

– Tap the “Add a new contact” icon, and then choose how you want to share your “invitation twincode”: “In-person” if the person is near you physically, or with a “Social” application, such as Message, Mail, or a social network otherwise.

– When receiving a twinme invitation twincode, from scanning its “QR-Code” with twinme, or from opening with twinme the web address received by email, text, chat, SMS or any other means, you can “Accept” or “Reject” the invitation. You will be able to Edit later the specific profile (name and image) you want to share with this particular contact (see below).

At the end of this process, you and your contact share a unique relation, which cannot be used by anyone else, and are under full control of each end.

Contacts

Tap a contact entry in your twinme contact list to:

– “Select” the type of communication you want to use: chat messages, voice call or video call;

– “Edit” your name and image representing you to each particular contact;

– “Edit” your contact local name.

When you change your image, the new one is pushed to your contact’s device and appears in his/her contact list.

If you change your name, the new one is pushed to your contact’s device and appears as your default local name when your contact wants to edit it.

When you “Remove a Contact”, you appear as anonymous in his/her contact list, and you cannot communicate with each other’s anymore.
